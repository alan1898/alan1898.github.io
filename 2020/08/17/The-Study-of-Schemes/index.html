A CPA-Secure PKE scheme based on the DBDH problem

For an instance of the DBDH problem $(g,g^a,g^b,g^c,T)$, the task is to determine whether $T$ is equal to $e(g,g)^{abc}$ or is selected uniformly from $\mathbb{G}$. We can set the ciphertext as $T\cdot m$, and the “random-looking” value $T=e(g,g)^{abc}=e(g^a,g^b)^c=e(g_1,g_2)^r$ (in the real world).

Construction

  • Setup$(1^\lambda)\rightarrow pp$
  • KeyGen$(pp)\rightarrow (pk,sk)$
  • Encrypt$(m\in\mathbb{G}_T,pk)$
  • Decrypt$(CT=(C_1,C_2),sk)$
